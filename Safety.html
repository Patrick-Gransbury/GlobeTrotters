GlobeTrotters Safety Tips


GlobeTrotters is a website that allows you to reach out and connect with fellow travellers online. However, meeting new people online can be very dangerous - so we have created this page to help you online in case you have any problems or queries. We do not have a professional support system, but want to make sure your safe so if you have further questions please seek the relvant Support or Advice at the bottom of this page.


Make sure you are safe when using our website online, and when meeting up with people you have matched with!


Tips for Online Safety


  • Report Any Suspicious or Offensive Behaviour

    It is important that if someone crosses the line, we know. We can protect you and other users with your help, so speak out if you see anything suspicious. Examples of fraudulent users are:

    • Users Lying about their Age
    • Users pushing to meet or avoiding questions
    • Users asking for personal information
    • Harassment or Threats
    • Discrimination of any kind
    • Commercial or unrecognisable links or attempts to sell products

  • Protect your Information

    Be careful about what you say online. Avoid giving out information like your home address, daily routine, job/ workplace, family names etc, with people you do not know. Keep your personal life to yourself - you are using our app to meet people to go travelling with!

  • Never Share Financial Information

    Financial scam artits are everywhere! Never give out your bank account information and don't send money to other users even if they suggest they are in an emergency. If someone tries to force you to send money, Block and Report them immediately!

    For Advice and Support on Online scams and finance Safety, visit www.citizensadvice.org.uk

  • Protect your Account

    Identity fraud can be a real threat. Make sure you don't share your username or passwords with anyone. When you choose your password, try and create a new password with a mixture of random lower-case and upper-case letters, numbers and special chracters. Always be careful when logging into your account using a public or shared-computer. If you receive an email asking for your username and password, report it immediately! - We will never send you an email asking for your username/ password.


Tips for Meeting in Person


  • Before you Meet

    Take your time getting to know people and don't rush! They will probably be just as cautious about meeting new people so make sure you get to know the other person before agreeing to meet them. Ask plenty of questions and screen for red flags - a perfect way of getting to know someone is to phone or video call them before meeting.

  • Meet in Public

    Meet for the first few times in a populated, public area - if you are pressured into going to a private location or their home, Decline and Report them.

  • Tell a Friend

    Before meeting with someone, be sure to tell a friend or family member where, when and who you are meeting. Make sure your mobile is charged and ready to use at all times. Don't change plans when meeting so your friends/ family always know where you are.

  • Your Limits

    If you are meeting for a drink - be aware of the affect of drugs and alcohol on you - they can impare your judgment, and alertness. If you feel presurised to take drugs or drink more than your comfortable with, hold your ground and end the meeting.

  • Transportation

    Be in control of your own transportation. Plan how you are going to get to and from the location you met at. Don't accept a private ride and don't tell the other person where you live/ how your getting home (espeically if your walking).

  • Don't Leave Anything Unattended

    Make sure no personal items are left unattended - laptops and mobiles can be expensive so only take what is neccessary on your date. Make sure you keep your wallet on your person at all times. Only accept drinks served directly from the bartender/sever, do not leave your drink unattended. Many substances are odourless, tasteless and colourless so be careful.

  • If you are Uncomfortable, Leave

    It is ok to leave early, especially if you feel uncomfortable. If you have a bad feeling or you feel unsafe, ask a bartender/ server for help.

  • Discrimination

    If you feel discriminated against or racially profiled, leave immediately ask a friend for help and support and let us know. We can remove their account and make sure it doesn't happen to others!


Tips for Travelling


  • Do your Research

    Get to know your destination in depth before you arrive. Currencies can change from country to country, and some countries use many currencies. Laws may be different to what your used to so make sure you know the main ones. Make use of friends, family and traveler reviews. They can give tips on places to visit, safer neighborhoods, and how much money you might need.

  • Make Copies of Important Documents

    You never know when you might need a copy of your passport, driver's license or another form of identification. Scan these documents and save online and print out hard copies. That way if you go on a trip you can take these expendable copies instead of loosing the originals.

  • Know Who you are Going With

    Since you might be going travelling with people you met on this website, make sure you have spent sufficient time getting to know them and have met them numerous times in person.

  • Keep Friends and Family Updated

    Again, relating to the fact you might be travelling with people you haven't known for very long, make sure your family/ friends are aware of your whereabouts and you keep them updates as to how the trip is going. Before you leave, send a copy of your plans to a few trusted people who can keep tabs on your whereabouts.

  • Keep your Valuables Safe

    If you are taking valuable jewllery/ tech, make sure you are staying in accomdation with a sufficient safe, and insurance. Never take anything with you that you aren't prepared to loose! Make sure you take a checklist of everything you brought with you, so you can check you have everyting when you leave.

  • Protect Yourself with Travel Insurance

    If you are unsure, talk to someone that has been on holiday alone before - a friend or a family member. Travel Insurance can cover you on many basis from loosing valuables, medical emergencies or cancelling your trip in its entirety. Get a few quotes and make sure you get Travel Insurance that best covers you!

  • Have Fun!

    Make sure you have a fun itinerary planned and you have plenty of money. Always take spare cash for emergencies!
